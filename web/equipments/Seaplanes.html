The formula used to compute the anti-ship DPS values of the bombers below (against Light, Medium and Heavy armour) is as follows:

[math] \begin{align} Damage per Second &= \frac{\sum_i^N damage(Bomb_i) \times armourmod(Bomb_i)}{RoF \times 2.2 + Absolute Cooldown} \end{align} [/math]

The formula used to compute the anti-air DPS values of the bombers below is as follows:

[math] \begin{align} Damage per Second &= \frac{\sum_i^N \frac{damage(AAGun_i)}{reload(AAGun_i)}}{RoF \times 2.2 + Absolute Cooldown} \end{align} [/math]

Usage Notes:

1. The constants used for bomb and gun damage are as follows:

Absolute Airstrike Cooldown = 0.1 seconds.

2. Bomb calculations somewhat unrealistically assume all bombs hit exactly one target. Values in practice are different when bombs hit multiple targets at once or miss completely, and the probability of this happening depends on the bomb explosion radius to aiming error margin ratio, as well as the number of targets in the bomb area.

3. As AA performance depends on how many aircraft your aircraft have to shoot to begin with, two indicators are provided.

  • AA Gun DPS assumes aircraft have an unlimited number of targets to shoot at, and predicts performance of planes against **multiple waves** of enemy aircraft.
  • AA Gun Burst assumes every gun gets to fire **only once**, and predicts performance of planes against a **single wave** of enemy aircraft per launch.

4. Bombers will prioritize damage on humanoids target instead of other enemies.. Timing airstrikes properly greatly affects damage achieved.

5. All the values used do not factor in the Reload or Airpower stats.

  • The Reload stat affects only Reload, and does not affect the Absolute Cooldown, so relative DPS rankings may change at higher Reload stats.
  • On ships with lower base Airpower, guns with higher Airpower stat but lower DPS may perform better than those with higher DPS but lower Airpower stat.
