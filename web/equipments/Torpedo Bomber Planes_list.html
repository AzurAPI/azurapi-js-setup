The formula used to compute the anti-ship DPS values of the torpedo bombers below is as follows:

[math] \begin{align} \mathrm{Damage\ per\ Second} &= \dfrac{\mathrm{Damage\ per\ Torpedo(Dmg)} \times \mathrm{Number\ of\ Torpedoes(Rnd)} \times \mathrm{Armour\ Modifier}}{\mathrm{RoF} \times 2.2 + \mathrm{Absolute\ Cooldown}} \end{align} [/math]

The formula used to compute the anti-air DPS values of the torpedo bombers below is as follows:

[math] \begin{align} \mathrm{Damage\ per\ Second} &= \dfrac{\sum_i^N {\mathrm{damage(AAGun_i)}}/{\mathrm{reload(AAGun_i)}}}{\mathrm{RoF} \times 2.2 + \mathrm{Absolute\ Cooldown}} \end{align} [/math]

The formula used to compute the anti-air Burst values of the torpedo bombers below is as follows:

[math] \begin{align} \mathrm{Damage\ per\ Second} &= \dfrac{\sum_i^N \mathrm{damage(AAGun_i)}}{\mathrm{RoF} \times 2.2 + \mathrm{Absolute\ Cooldown}} \end{align} [/math]

Usage Notes:

1. The constants used for torpedo and gun damage are as follows:

Absolute Airstrike Cooldown = 0.1 seconds.

2. As AA performance depends on how many aircraft your aircraft have to shoot to begin with, two indicators are provided.

  • AA Gun DPS assumes aircraft have an unlimited number of targets to shoot at, and predicts performance of planes against multiple waves of enemy aircraft.
  • AA Gun Burst assumes every gun gets to fire only once, and predicts performance of planes against a single wave of enemy aircraft per launch.

3. Japanese torpedo bombers tend to unload on the closest targets to the centre of the screen and ignore the ones further left or right completely. All other torpedo bombers tend to launch their torpedoes in parallel patterns near the centre of the screen (and may completely miss a target if it is located at the extremities). Timing airstrikes properly greatly affects damage achieved.

4. All the values used do not factor in the Reload or Airpower stats.

  • The Reload stat affects only Reload, and does not affect the Absolute Cooldown, so relative DPS rankings may change at higher Reload stats.
  • On ships with lower base Airpower, guns with higher Airpower stat but lower DPS may perform better than those with higher DPS but lower Airpower stat.
